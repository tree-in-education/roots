Facial Recognition & Law Enforcement

In the United States, facial recognition use in law enforcement is becoming more and more prevalent. As you’ve learned, facial recognition only works when you have a database of faces to compare to. Government agencies have access to a lot of faces. In many states, law enforcement officials are given freedom to use driver’s license photo databases. In other states, mugshot databases are used. Police collect mugshots from people they have arrested, then compare them to local, state, and federal face databases. Facial recognition is used by law enforcement in Chicago, New York, Los Angeles, and other cities in the US. In some cities, like San Francisco, law enforcement has been banned from using face recognition technology. Civil liberty groups have expressed concern, citing the potential for the technology to be used to provide governments with unprecedented power to track individuals.
