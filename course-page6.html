Facial Comparison

Once an algorithm has encoded a face using a deep learning algorithm, it is ready to be compared to other faces. The similarity between two images can be calculated by finding the distance between their metrics. Two similar faces will have metrics that are close to one another, and two dissimilar faces will have metrics that are far apart.

Comparing one face to another in a facial recognition system is called face verification. It is used to power services like Apple’s Face ID, which lets you unlock your iPhone with your face. It compares the face structure metrics it finds in your face and compares them to an image of your face on your phone. If your face’s metrics are similar enough, the phone will recognize you as yourself, and your phone will unlock.

The other type of facial recognition system is face identification. Face identification involves comparing one face with many other faces in order to determine the identity of a face. Face identification systems must have a facial database, which stores the data of many faces. Facial databases can store the data of millions of different faces. In order for a face to be recognized by a facial recognition system, its metrics have to be a close match to one of the faces in the database.
