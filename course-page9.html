Algorithmic Bias

Machine learning has limitations. The algorithm learns nothing more than what it finds in the data. The effectiveness of a face encoding algorithm is reliant on the size and diversity of it’s database. And though most face databases used to train these algorithms are sufficiently large, they are rarely sufficiently diverse. In a study published in December 2019, the National Institute of Standards and Technology found that facial recognition false positives (times the algorithm was wrong about a “match”) were 10 to 100 times more likely when analyzing faces of non-european descent. This is a direct result of algorithms trained unbalanced databases that aren’t inclusive of all types of appearance.
