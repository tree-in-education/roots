Facial Landmark Estimation

The next step in the facial recognition process is identification facial features. Facial features include the mouth, eyes, nose, and outline of the jaw. The algorithm that finds your facial features is called face landmark estimation. This algorithm can be used to highlight your facial features in an image. These algorithms generally identify 68 points on the face as facial landmarks.

Face landmark estimation is a popular tool outside of facial recognition. For example, it is the method that Snapchat and other apps use to make filters that interact with your facial features.

In the facial recognition process, facial landmark estimation is used to do face alignment, which is used to correct for head rotation in the image. This means the image will be shifted and rotated so that the mouth and eyes are centered in the image. This is how facial recognition algorithms work on faces that are not directly facing the camera.
